194000. The Doped and Deposed Prince

Rajputana — Not random

Conditions

  • Monarch Ranmalla is active

Will happen within 100 days of January 2, 1435
Checked again every 100 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after December 30, 1438)

Description

In 1438 Prince Rainmal, one of the many Rajput princes, had long had his eye on a nearby strategic fort destined for his nephew but court ettiquete and intrigue kept him from his goal. 194206. The Fall of Mandu

Rajputana — Not random

Conditions

  • Mughal Empire controls Ujjain

Will happen within 30 days of January 2, 1542
Checked again every 30 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after November 2, 1567)

Description

Thanks to its position between the Gujarat Sultans and the Mughals, Malwa was conquered twice, by Sher Shah in 1542 and by the Mughals in 1561. The King of Malwa, Baz Bahadur, was forced to flee his capital of Mandu and arrived at Chittor, capital of the powerful Rajput Kingdom of Mewar. King Rana Sanga welcomed him to his court, an action which angered the Great Mughal but kept the shaky Rajput alliance alive.

Actions

A. Give refuge to Baz Bahadur

  • Cede Ujjain to Mughal Empire
  • -25 relations with Mughal Empire
  • +1 diplomats

B. Every man for himself!

  • Cede Ujjain to Mughal Empire
  • Stability -2
  • Global revolt risk +5 for 12 months

194211. The Fall of Chittorgarh

Rajputana — Not random

Conditions

  • Mughal Empire and Rajputana are at war
  • Mughal Empire controls Bikaner

Will happen within 30 days of October 2, 1567
Checked again every 30 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after October 26, 1605)

Description

Despite fierce resistance from Rajput nobles the capital of Mewar fell in 1568. The capture of the city broke Rajput resistance and soon afterwards the remaining states that had refused to ally with Akbar submitted to his rule. In 1569 Rai Sarjana Hara of Ranthambhor and Raja Ramchand of Bundelkhand surrendered and in 1570 the rulers of Bikaner and Jaisalmer also submitted, giving their daughters in marriage. Despite this crushing blow, the King of Mewar refused to submit and ruled over an independent rump from Kumbhalgarh.

Actions

A. Set up an independent state in Kumbhalgarh

  • Move capital to Marwar
  • Cede Bikaner to Mughal Empire
  • Stability -4

B. Become a puppet of the Mughals

  • Event 230209 - Mewar submits to Mughal rule for Mughal Empire is triggered immediately
  • Stability -2
  • +100 relations with Mughal Empire
  • Event 194212 - The Coronation of Maharana Pratap for Rajputana will never fire

194213. Mughal envoys arrive

Rajputana — Not random

Conditions

  • Mughal Empire exists
  • The following must not occur:
    • Rajputana is a vassal of Mughal Empire

Will happen within 90 days of January 2, 1573
Checked again every 90 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after October 26, 1605)

Description

Having gone to war twice to conquer Mewar, Akbar changed tactics and sent a series of envoys to Pratap with a peace treaty. Although the King was amenable to peace, he adamantly refused to become a vassal of the Mughals and demanded that Mewar retain its independence. Frustrated and humiliated, Akbar gave the order to destroy Mewar once and for all. On May 3, 1573 the Mughals marched towards Kumbhalgarh.

  • -50 relations with Mughal Empire
  • +2000 infantry in the capital province
  • Event 230214 - The Pratap spurns peace for Mughal Empire is triggered immediately

B. Peace on any terms

  • +50 relations with Mughal Empire
  • Event 230215 - Peace at last for Mughal Empire is triggered immediately

194216. The Battle of Haldighati

Rajputana — Not random

Conditions

  • The following must not occur:
    • Rajputana is a vassal of Mughal Empire
  • Mughal Empire controls Marwar
  • Rajputana owns Marwar

Will happen within 1 days of March 4, 1576
Checked again every 1 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after October 26, 1605)
unless prevented by
Action B of 194212 - The Coronation of Maharana Pratap for Rajputana

Description

Just after dawn on June 18, 1576, the Mughal army crossed the river near Khamnor and attacked Pratrap Singh's capital of Kumbhalgarh. The canny Rajputs used their knowledge of the mountainous passes to good use and, allied with the Bhils, fought the Mughals to a standstill for four hours, slaying the notorious Rajput traitor Man Singh. In the end, however, the Rajputs broke and Pratrap Singh fled to lead the resistance from the mountains. Although the battle was a success for the Mughals, it showed the tenacity of the Rajput fighters and is still remembered as one of their greatest moments.

Actions

A. We will never surrender!

  • Marwar revolts
