163000. Ireland

Province of Pale — Not random

Conditions

  • The following must not occur:
    • Ireland exists
  • Own Pale
  • Control Pale
  • At least one of the following must occur:
    • All of the following must occur:
      • Own Connachta
      • Own Aileach
    • All of the following must occur:
      • Own Connachta
      • Own Cill Dara
    • All of the following must occur:
      • Own Connachta
      • Own Mhumhain
    • All of the following must occur:
      • Own Aileach
      • Own Cill Dara
    • All of the following must occur:
      • Own Cill Dara
      • Own Mhumhain
  • At least one of the following must occur:
    • Connaught owns Pale
    • Leinster owns Pale
    • Desmond owns Pale
    • Ulster owns Pale
  • Country is not at war

Will happen within 30 days of January 2, 1419
Checked again every 30 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after December 31, 1819)

Description

We have freed this island and unified it. We can now claim the title of High King of Ireland!

Actions

A. Long live Ireland!

  • +200 victory points
  • Stability -2
  • Move capital to Pale
  • +1000 population in Pale
  • Flag graphics set to default
  • Become Ireland
  • Mhumhain will be considered a national province
  • Connachta will be considered a national province
  • Cill Dara will be considered a national province
  • Laighin will be considered a national province
  • Pale will be considered a national province
  • Aileach will be considered a national province
  • Ulaidh will be considered a national province
  • Centralization +1
  • Aristocracy +1
  • Global revolt risk +5 for 60 months

B. Let us not upset the old clannish ways

  • -100 victory points
  • Centralization -1
  • Stability +1

3829. Hugh O'Neill's Irish Rebellion

Ireland — Not random

Conditions

  • Event 3022 - Hugh O'Neill's Irish Rebellion for England has already occurred
  • The following must not occur:
    • England is a vassal of Ireland

Will happen within 60 days of January 2, 1592
Checked again every 60 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after December 30, 1599)
unless prevented by
Action B of 338082 - King Henry's Offer for Ulaidh

Description

Our agreement with Queen Elizabeth has done little for Ireland. Indeed, English influence has only increased and the power of our own noblemen has been severely curtailed. The people and the nobility cry out for freedom from the English tyranny. Shall we rise up and throw them out?

Actions

A. Yes, we shall be free!

  • -200 relations with England
  • +100 relations with Spain
  • Gain a temporary casus belli against England for 60 months
  • Break vassalization with England
  • +2000 infantry in a random province
  • +1000 cavalry in a random province

B. No, the English are too strong

  • +100 relations with England
  • Stability -1
  • Centralization +1

Conditions

  • Country is not at war
  • Own Mhumhain
  • Own Connachta
  • Own Cill Dara
  • Own Laighin
  • Own Pale
  • Own Aileach
  • Own Ulaidh
  • Control Mhumhain
  • Control Connachta
  • Control Cill Dara
  • Control Laighin
  • Control Pale
  • Control Aileach
  • Control Ulaidh
  • Stability is at 3 or higher
  • None of the following must occur:
    • Ireland is a vassal of England
    • Ireland is a vassal of Scotland
    • Ireland is a vassal of Wales
    • Ireland is a vassal of France
    • Ireland is a vassal of Spain
    • Ireland is a vassal of Aragon
    • Ireland is a vassal of Castile
    • Ireland is a vassal of Portugal
    • Ireland is a vassal of Burgundy
    • Ireland is a vassal of Denmark
    • Ireland is a vassal of Sweden
    • Ireland is a vassal of Norway
    • Ireland is a vassal of Brittany

Will happen within 30 days of January 2, 1650
Checked again every 30 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after December 31, 1819)

Description

With the expulsion of all foreign influence within Ireland, the new Irish state would have to decide whether to centralize authority in Dublin, or to remain as a loose alliance of feudal lords and clan leaders.

Actions

A. Centralize the Government in Dublin

  • Move capital to Pale
  • +1 base tax value in Pale
  • Centralization +1
  • Event 20606 - A Question of Laws for Ireland is triggered immediately

B. Leave Authority with the Earls and Clans

  • Aristocracy +2
  • Centralization -2
  • Pale will be considered a national province

20606. A Question of Laws

Ireland — Not random

Triggered by

Action A of 20605 - The Foundation of an Irish State for Ireland

Description

Due to the influence of the English, Ireland had long had a mixture of Breton law and English Common Law. This caused significant confusion and disruptions, particularly with respect to inheritances. One of the first priorities of the new Irish government in Dublin would be to establish a coherent law code, but a good portion of the country would be violently angry no matter which way they went.

Actions

A. English Common Law

  • Stability -3
  • Aristocracy +1
  • Centralization +1
  • Mhumhain revolts
  • Connachta revolts
  • Aileach revolts
  • Ulaidh revolts

B. Breton Law

  • Stability -3
  • Serfdom -1
  • Centralization -1
  • Mhumhain revolts
  • Cill Dara revolts
  • Laighin revolts
  • Pale revolts
