441000. Destruction of the Sete traders

Angoche — Not random

Conditions

  • At least one of the following must occur:
    • Portugal owns Collasuyu
    • Portugal controls Collasuyu
    • Event 260113 - Barreto-Homem Expedition for Portugal has already occurred

Will happen within 100 days of July 2, 1572
Checked again every 100 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after January 2, 1579)

Description

The Portuguese massacre of the Muslim trading community at Sete destroyed Angoche's access to the gold trade with Monomotapa. From now on it would depend on ivory from areas not controlled by the Portuguese.

Actions

A. Damn!

  • -2 merchants
  • Trade tech investment: -500

441001. Angoche burnt by the Portuguese

Angoche — Not random

Triggered by

Action A of 260115 - Angoche has resisted our efforts to monopolize trade for Portugal

Description

Irritated by the resistance of Angoche's merchants to the Portuguese efforts to establish a monopoly over trade in Southeastern Africa, the Governor-General of India, Afonso de Albuquerque, ordered the town razed to the ground as an object lesson. Ant—Énio de Saldanha, Captain of Sofala sailed on 1 July 1511 with some 60 men and burnt Angoche despite 1200 defenders.

Actions

A. Damn!

  • -50
  • -2 merchants
  • Trade tech investment: -500
  • -100 relations with Portugal
