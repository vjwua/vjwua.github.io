Since 1646 there were economic relations between the British East India Company and Oman. When Napolйon sought to challenge British commercial and political activities around the Indian ocean by his Egyptian campaign, the British government reacted to the threat by offering the Omani Sultan Sвоd I a closer cooperation. Sвоd agreed, and in 1798 and 1800 two treaties were signed.
