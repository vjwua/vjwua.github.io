274000. The Rule of Inal

Circassia — Not random

Will happen within 360 days of January 2, 1427
Checked again every 360 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after January 2, 1428)

Description

For centuries, Circassia was divided between princes and no organised structure was developed. In the early 1400s, Inal Nekhu moved to Circassia to unite it with his power. While Circassian lordships fell into Inal's hands one by one, he fought and defeated warlords and clan chieftains. A confederation of 30 Circassian clans tried to prevent Inal's rise, but in a decisive battle near the Mzymta River, he managed to defeat the combined army of thirty major feudal lords. Thanks to his conquests in the Kuban and Abkhazia, as well as his administrative and military changes, Inal was nicknamed the Great.

Actions

A. Circassia is united

  • Change the city name in Adiga to Shanjir
  • +1 base tax value in Adiga
  • Centralization +3
  • Land +1

274003. Adoption of Islam

Circassia — Not random

Conditions

  • None of the following must occur:
    • State religion is sunni
    • State religion is shia
  • At least one of the following must occur:
    • Crimea is a neighbor
    • Golden Horde is a neighbor
    • Astrakhan is a neighbor
    • Ottoman Empire is a neighbor

Will happen within 360 days of January 2, 1730
Checked again every 360 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after January 2, 1780)

Description

Orthodoxy was spread among Circassians, but they did not abandon all elements of their indigenous religious beliefs, called Khabzeism. The prominent concepts of Khabzeism include honour (nape), manifestation of compassion (guschlegu), gratuitous help (psape), which were supposed to ensure that the human soul could join the soul of the ancestors with a clear conscience (nape huzhkle). Together with the Adyghe Khabze moral code, it was dominant in Circassia to the 18th century. Under the influence of Crimean Tatars and Turks, large numbers of Circassians converted to Islam from Christianity. This was facilitated by the destruction of Christian churches and the efforts of Ottoman imams and Sufi preachers. While the Circassian court and nobles were officially Sunni Muslims, most Circassians retained their beliefs.

  • Stability +1

274006. Commoners Uprisings

Circassia — Not random

Will happen within 2000 days of January 2, 1770
Checked again every 2000 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after January 2, 1790)

Description

In 1770, the free farmers of Abzakh Circassians revolted against the aristocratic class. Captured princes were killed. Those who escaped took refuge in other Circassian tribes. All privileges held by the princes, who were aristocratic and noble class, were abolished by free farmers. Similar unrest later occurred among Natukhaj, Shapsugs and Abedzeks tribes, who formed the majority of the Circassians.

274007. Imam of Caucausus

Circassia — Not random

Conditions

  • At least one of the following must occur:
    • State religion is sunni
    • State religion is shia

Will happen within 360 days of January 2, 1786
Checked again every 360 days until trigger is met (cannot happen after January 2, 1787)

Description

Mansur Ushurma came from a Chechen teip and became an imam in 1784. When he returned to Chechnya, he became upset by the Russian encroachment in the North Caucasus. He ordered the remaining non-Muslim Chechens to follow Islamic precepts, and by declaring a holy war he sought to ensure unity among the Chechen teips. In 1786, Sheikh Mansur arrived in Circassia to lead Circassians against Russia and strengthen Islamic practice there. In 1791, the imam was captured and sent to St Petersburg, where he died in April 1794.
